[1] Introduction

1.1 Motivation

  • The visually impaired face significant challenges to independent indoor navigation.
  • Existing navigation aids for blind individuals are often limited in functionality or require specialized infrastructure.
  • A fusion of LiDAR and camera data can provide a robust and comprehensive understanding of the surrounding environment, enabling safer and more intuitive indoor navigation.

1.2 Scope

  • This project focuses on developing an iPhone application that utilizes LiDAR and camera data for indoor navigation assistance for visually impaired individuals.
  • The application shall provide real-time spatial awareness, object detection, and guidance information.
  • The scope is limited to indoor environments and does not encompass outdoor navigation.

1.3 Objectives

  • Develop an iPhone application that integrates LiDAR and camera sensor data.
  • Implement algorithms for environment mapping, object detection, and obstacle avoidance.
  • Design an intuitive user interface for navigating and interacting with the application.
  • Provide audio and haptic feedback for clear and accessible guidance.
  • Conduct user testing to evaluate the application's effectiveness and usability.

1.4 Success Criteria

  • Successful navigation through various indoor environments without collisions or significant user effort.
  • Positive feedback from target users regarding ease of use, intuitiveness, and accuracy.
  • Achievement of pre-defined performance metrics for obstacle detection, distance estimation, and path planning.

[4] WRS

4.1 W

4.1.1 Stakeholders

Our application is designed to aid visually impaired individuals by providing real-time indoor navigation assistance, enabling them to move confidently and independently. It is developed and maintained by the development team, ensuring the app meets accessibility standards, user needs, and adheres to all of the requirements (both functional and nonfunctional). Family members and caregivers benefit from the app as it helps their loved ones navigate safely. Healthcare providers can recommend the app to visually impaired patients as a tool to enhance their independence. Additionally, the app complies with iPhone App Store guidelines, ensuring it is accessible and available to users worldwide.

Of the people

  • Investors
  • iOS App Store

By the people

  • Development Team
  • Architect Team
  • Requirements Team
  • Deployment Team

For the people

  • Visually Impaired
  • Healthcare Providers
  • Caregivers
  • Family Members of Visually Impaired

4.1.2 Problem

Visually impaired individuals face significant challenges when navigating indoor environments, as many existing navigation tools are designed primarily for outdoor use and rely on visual cues. This lack of accessible, real-time indoor navigation solutions can lead to a loss of independence, increased reliance on caregivers, and limited access to public spaces.

4.1.3 Goal

The goal of this project is to develop a highly accessible, user-friendly iPhone app that enables visually impaired individuals to navigate indoor environments independently and confidently. The app will provide real-time guidance using LiDAR technology and spatial mapping, designed with a focus on accessibility and usability. By delivering accurate, reliable navigation assistance, the app aims to enhance independence and improve the quality of life for visually impaired users.

4.1.4 Improved Understanding of the Domain, Stakeholders, Functional and Non-Functional Objectives

In order to achieve the goal of safety, many different situations must be considered. The same is true to ensure stakeholders can use the application easily and effectively. Both functional and nonfunctional objectives have been expounded upon to meet the goal of safety.

Index

Creeping Rate

  • The team shall handle a 10% change in requirements per month.
  • The exact measure of this percentage shall be based on the average of a group estimate held whenever changes are proposed.
  • All affected stakeholders and the development team must be present during these decisions.

Why Choose Our Solution?

  • Our project focuses on a specific technology called LiDAR. By focussing on this cutting-edge technology, we are able to ensure that all the requirements are met.
  • Other projects focus on GPS, but this technology is often poor indoors. We limit our reliance on this unreliable technology as much as possible.
  • We have extreme care for user experience. Our design is user-centric and focussed around the idea that our users are visually impaired. The core design is able to be used via voice commands or external assistance devices.
  • We have committed to supporting our app and increasing integrations with other applications and technologies, such as IOT assistance tools.
